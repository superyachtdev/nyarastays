Our Journal

A quieter kind of stay.

Nyara began the way most small things do — with a feeling. That hotels had become too loud, too fluorescent, too eager to be everywhere at once.

N° 01 — Origin

A garden, a coffee, a decision.

The first Yume was a sketch on a napkin in Uluwatu. Two friends, one too-warm Sunday morning, and a small clifftop plot that nobody had touched. We built one villa. Painted it cream. Watched a banana tree grow through the bathroom window. Guests started arriving. They didn't want to leave. So we built a second villa. Then a third.

N° 02 — Principle

Stay long. Move slow. Eat well.

Every Nyara home is small on purpose. We won't build a hundredth. We'd rather you stayed five nights than one, and walked away knowing the name of a surf instructor, a fisherman, a beach with no sign. Bali and the south of Phuket have these in abundance. We simply point.

N° 03 — What's next

One door at a time.

Nyara Villas opened the year after — this time on Phuket, in Rawai. Two multi-level townhouse villas, side by side, big enough for the wedding-after-the-wedding, small enough to feel like home. We're not in a hurry. The next house will arrive when it should.

What we believe

Four small rules we live by.

  1. 01

    Less, but better.

    Two homes. One coastline. No third unless it earns the air.

  2. 02

    The host is the place.

    A steward who knows your name beats a lobby every time.

  3. 03

    The slow road wins.

    We send you down the lane, not up to a rooftop bar.

  4. 04

    Quiet is a feature.

    We design for the morning. The rest tends to itself.

Come and see.

The pages will tell you only so much.
